# 1990s Archaeological Findings at Tiwanaku

## Major 1990s Discoveries

### Proyecto Wila Jawira (Kolata & Rivera)
- Rediscovery and study of *suka kollus* (raised field agriculture)
- Evidence of drought and environmental stress
- Refined chronologies of Tiwanaku's occupation

### Akapana Pyramid Excavations
- Evidence of human sacrifice and violent burials from around 910 CE
- Signs of rebellion and burning around 1000 CE
- Discovery of kero-shattering rituals

### Underwater Archaeology (1988-1992)
- 385 artifacts recovered from Lake Titicaca
- Gold Staff God medallions and ceremonial offerings
- Evidence of ritual deposition in sacred waters

### Burial Practices
- Below-ground burials with elaborate textiles
- Warp-faced stripe patterns and polychrome weaving
- Modest social differentiation in grave goods

### Putuni Palace
- Evidence of burning during 1000 CE rebellion
- Signs of violent conflict and social upheaval
